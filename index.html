About the League

What is NCAL?

NCAL, or the North Carolina Academic League, is an organization dedicated to boosting the state, quality, and size of Quizbowl within North Carolina at the high school level. Currently based in Chapel Hill, NCAL aims to support teams from the Research Triangle and beyond by hosting regularly scheduled tournaments at a more affordable cost.

We are especially committed to supporting rural, Title 1, and underrepresented schools within North Carolina to play quizbowl and qualify for National level tournaments.

NCAL envisions a future where North Carolina contains both thriving local circuits, alongside a stronger, unified network stretching across the state.

What is Quiz Bowl?

Quiz Bowl is an academic competition comprising a wide variety of topics, ranging from trivia and popular culture to math and physics. The base concept is that two teams of 4 players each compete to answer questions, and whoever is able to score more points is the victor.

To discover more about its formal game mechanics, parameters, and structure, you can learn more here: About Quiz Bowl (NAQT).

Why is Quiz Bowl beneficial?

Quiz Bowl is a widespread activity that has connected people from all over the country over the shared pursuit of knowledge. Quiz Bowl helps students identify concepts of interest in a fun and accessible manner, while also promoting critical thinking under pressure and teamwork. Players need to learn how to communicate ideas with their team to find answers. Quiz Bowl also benefits from an incredibly inclusive and active community, with openly accessible ways to connect with others.

How can my school get involved?

Quiz Bowl takes a handful of motivated and interested students. It's beneficial to have a club where the students can convene and practice for tournaments. We recommend finding a teacher interested to serve as advisor and have students join the club.

How should I study?

There are many beneficial ways to study Quiz Bowl, and choosing the one for you can take time. As a team, its often very important to try to distribute categories across teammates, allowing for different people to focus on different subject areas. For NAQT questions, the subject distribution can be found here, allowing for you to determine levels of priority: NAQT High School Subject Distribution.

There are two "kinds" of Quiz Bowl knowledge, dubbed "real" and "fake." Real knowledge is considered knowledge gained through more organic means, where students study what interests them in traditional manners, such as classroom activities and textbooks. Fake knowledge is considered knowledge gained through specifically studying Quiz Bowl content. People study for fake knowledge through playing a large amount of questions (primarily through qbreader.org), and flashcards (such as the Anki application). Despite its name, fake knowledge is no worse than real knowledge.

NCAL Season Pass

Starting this year, we are offering an institutional NCAL Season Pass program to ease programmatic registration costs for new and growing teams across the state circuit.

Eligibility Breakdown

  • Newly Established Programs: Teams established starting from the 2026-2027 school year are eligible to register for any official NCAL tournament completely free.
  • Developing Programs with Need: Teams established starting from the 2025-2026 school year (at an institutional age of 2 years) with demonstrated financial or operational need are eligible to pay for a seasonal pass for a flat fee of $250 across all circuit events. This represents an estimated value of $540.

More information about the season pass can be found on the community forums.

Statistics Sharing Service

Want hands-on assistance uploading comprehensive full statistics reports for your next tournament event? We have your program covered from start to finish at zero operational cost.

Submission Instructions

Simply compile and transmit either of the following documentation variants to our administrative staff:

  • Clear legible images or scanned copies of physical paper scoresheets.
  • An accessible hyperlink routing to your program's active digital scoresheets.
Email files directly to: ncalquizbowl@gmail.com

The Result: Our staff will handle processing your recorded data to construct a fully standardized statistics file optimized and compatible with submission protocols for both the local forums and the official National Association for Quiz Bowl Tournaments (NAQT) website.
